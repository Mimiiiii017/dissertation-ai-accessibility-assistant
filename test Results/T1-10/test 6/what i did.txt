Justification for excluding phi4-reasoning:14b from the Cloud-LLM-Preliminary benchmark: 

phi4-reasoning:14b is installed and runs locally via Ollama but is not available through the cloud Ollama gateway used by the benchmark. The Cloud-LLM-Preliminary evaluation is designed to test models under identical network and infrastructure conditions — all models are routed through the same cloud endpoint, ensuring that latency, throughput, and availability are comparable across the set. Running phi4-reasoning locally would introduce a confounding variable: local inference operates on different hardware, under different memory constraints, and without the gateway's load-balancing behaviour, making its performance results incomparable with the cloud-hosted models. 

Additionally, the benchmark's composite scoring includes a speed component (20% weight). A locally-served model's response time reflects the researcher's local GPU/CPU capacity rather than the model's inherent efficiency, which would distort the ranking and undermine the validity of cross-model comparisons. 

If a cloud-routable variant of Phi-4 Reasoning becomes available through the gateway, it should be added at that point. Alternatively, it could be evaluated in a separate local-only benchmark run with adjusted methodology — for example, reporting latency separately and noting the hardware specification — so that results remain interpretable without conflating local and cloud inference conditions.
