Abstract

Abstract

Investors constantly strive to predict future market trends in order to maximize
their profits. However, the stock market’s highly volatile nature poses a significant challenge when it comes to accurately forecasting stock fluctuations. As a
result, successful investment strategies require a considerable investment of time
and effort in gathering and analyzing relevant information about specific stocks to
make well-informed decisions. This has generated significant interest within the
academic community regarding the use of machine learning methods for stock
market prediction.

While previous research in this field has shown promising results, it has yet to
meet the required standards for developing investor support tools that can make
optimal investment decisions on behalf of investors.

The proposed technology

addresses this by combining machine learning and natural language processing
techniques in a complementary manner. It incorporates an optimized data storage
engine that employs data serialization, memory caching, and index utilization to
achieve efficient query processing. Additionally, multiple crawling components are
utilized to extract freely available data from Yahoo Finance, including news articles, stock price history, and stock details. Moreover, the technology employs a
hybrid machine learning model that integrates weighting concepts from artificial
neural networks and clustering concepts from K-means classifiers. This approach
enhances the accuracy and effectiveness of the predictions.

In this research, a proof of concept tool was developed to evaluate the pro-

posed technology; specifically in predicting the 30-day outcome of 221 stocks
from the XETRA stock exchange. This study outperformed previous research endeavors that had achieved MSE error margins ranging from 0.00109 to 0.03526,

signifies a significant improvement and surpasses the earlier findings. The accomplishment of these results was made possible by utilizing 3 years of training
data, which enabled a thorough analysis and enhanced the accuracy of the predictions. These collective findings not only confirm the effectiveness of the proposed
technology but also establish a robust foundation for future explorations and advancements in this particular field.

1.1

Scope of Research

The stock market has a significant influence on both individual investors’ interests, as well as a country’s entire economy trend [1]. As a result, the prediction
of stock movement plays a vital role within the stock trading sphere. Investors
continuously attempt to anticipate future market trends in order to maximize profits [2]. However, the prediction of stock fluctuations is widely recognized as a
formidable task due to the stock market’s highly volatile nature [1] [3]. Several
key factors may affect the stock price of a particular company, such as financial
statements, breaking news stories, sector performance, market sentiment, etc. [4].
Therefore, successful investment strategies require a substantial investment of time
and effort in order to gather and analyze relevant information regarding specific
stocks, in order to make informed decisions [5]. Technical analysis and Fundamental analysis are two of the main methodologies that financial practitioners
employ when making trading decisions and forecasting stock prices, some combining the both to obtain increased benefit [6].

Advancement in machine learning technology in the field of stock market prediction has provided investors with trading support tools to assist them in their
stock trading ventures [7]. This is particularly useful for retail investors who are

2.1

Overview

This study aims to evaluate the performance of machine learning when applied to
technical and fundamental strategies in price movement prediction. Hence, literature regarding the following topics was reviewed: (a) types of investment products, (b) forms of quantitative and qualitative techniques used to anticipate market
trends and (c) the machine learning algorithms utilized in aiding trading decisions.

2.2

Stock Market

The stock market is a network of stock exchanges where investors exchange
stocks; assets that represent fractional ownership in a company. Like any other
economic market, the stock market employs fundamental economic principles such
as supply and demand, short selling, trading time frames, charting techniques, and
so on [5]. Trading stocks entails purchasing shares of a company and selling
them on a stock exchange to make a profit. This exchange benefits both corporations and investors, as the company’s value is directly proportional to value of
a stockholder’s shares [5]. A stock market index is a subset of the stock mar-

ket, used to gauge the performance of a country, region, industry or category of
stocks. Therefore indices are influential within the stock market as they measure
stocks collectively and thus track how the overall sub-market is performing [12].

2.3

Foreign Exchange

Foreign exchange (Forex) is the term used to describe the conversion of one currency to another, and it represents one of the largest financial markets in the
world. Hence, traders attempt to anticipate exchange movement trends in order to
maximize returns and minimize risks [13]. However, similarly to stocks, the exchange rate is influenced by a variety of factors, including a country’s economy,
politics, inter-market relationships, and so forth, thus making the forecasting of
Forex trends difficult [14] [13].

2.4

Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrencies are digital currencies traded among individuals or groups, using
network-based technology that employs cryptographic algorithms to secure transactions [15]. They possess characteristics of decentralization, transparency, and
immutability due to their reliance on blockchain technology [15]. Their prices
are highly volatile and influenced by factors such as transaction costs, mining
difficulty, market trends, popularity, alternative coin prices, stock markets, sentiments, and legal considerations [16]. These factors contribute to rapid fluctuations
in cryptocurrency prices, posing challenges for researchers attempting to forecast
them [16].

2.5

Trading and Investment Tools

Existing research shows that machine learning methods can make use of a company’s stock data to extract key trends and tentatively predict the direction of
the financial market [17]. These implementations utilized both technical analysis (which examines past stock prices, volumes and other parameters in order to
identify trends) and fundamental analysis (which examines the performance and
state of the company behind the stock to predict how the company will perform
in the future). Both are necessary to anticipate future market trends and price
accordingly [18].

2.5.1

Technical Analysis

Technical analysis is a technique that involves looking at stock prices’ past performance, trading patterns and other related elements. This analysis is based on
the principle that history repeats itself, and it can be used to predict market direction in the future [19]. Such forecasts could make use of data such as price,
volume and interest rates. While these mechanisms cannot predict the exact price
at which a stock will trade at any given time, they can identify trends and provide insight into whether it will rise or fall and at what rate [20]. However,
Technical Analysis only considers quantifiable information and neglects several
factors such as various forms of news, conferences and discussions, all of which
can influence investors’ attitudes and sentiment toward the market as a whole, or
specific stocks. These unrelated factors are difficult to predict, but they have the
potential to influence market trends and thus stock prices [21].

2.5.2

Fundamental Analysis

Fundamental analysis is a technique in which the factors which affect the stock
price, such as the performance of an organization and the profitability generated,
are thoroughly examined.

Fundamental analysis factors include data from the

economy (inflation, unemployment, debt level, etc.), the industry sector (including
competitors and partners/suppliers) and the company itself (earnings, management
effectiveness, returns on equity) [10] [22]. By evaluating these influential factors,
the relative value of a company’s shares could be estimated. The stock price
valuation can be used to determine whether the stock is at an acceptable price
and is not over or undervalued. Furthermore, by understanding an organization’s
share price, one can predict the expected returns on investment and make a more
informed investment decision [20].

2.6

Stock Market Prediction Techniques

Previous studies in this field primarily focused on the prediction of stock price
movement (identifies whether prices exhibit a bullish or bearish trend), rather than
attempting to predict the actual price [2]. Despite several scientific attempts, no
method has attained a level of accuracy in stock price prediction that could serve
as a substitute for human reasoning. However, despite the lack of consistent predictions, there have been some mild successes [4]. Stock market research encapsulates the two trading philosophies mentioned above; fundamental and technical
approaches. Technicians utilize charts and modeling techniques in order to identify patterns in price and volume. These individuals rely solely on historical data
to forecast future outcomes [19].

However, one area of limited success comes from qualitative information. Data
extracted from quarterly reports, news stories or online forums can drastically af8

fect the share price of a stock [4]. The majority of existing financial text mining
(the process of extracting valuable insights from textual data) literature relies on
the use of Natural Language Processing (NLP). NLP is a key tool in sentiment
analysis as it enables machines to interpret human language [23]. Named Entity
Recognition (NER) is a sub-field of NLP that involves identifying, extracting and
classifying named entities from text. Hence, using this technology, researchers are

2.6.1

Pattern Recognition

Pattern recognition is a technical analysis technique that involves the use of algorithms to identify recurring stock market patterns from stock price history [3].

that can be used to predict future market movements by identifying bullish (uptrend) flag patterns on the NASDAQ index. The researchers [9] created a methodology that combines two established pattern recognition techniques, perceptually

template matching and PIP pattern recognition techniques, have shown to be particularly effective in this area [8] [9].

2.6.2

Financial News Analysis

It has been established that a correlation exists between the publication of financial news and stock price fluctuations. Hence, research works exist on the analy10

Chapter 2. Literature Review

sis of financial news articles and other textual sources to extract insights, which
are then used to predict future price movements, with the use of NLP [27] [28].

Several studies have investigated the effectiveness of this fundamental analysis
approach, with promising results. For instance, a study by Guo [1] proposed
an Long short-term memory (LSTM) neural network model with news sentiment
scores and historical stock data as input, to predict future stock close price. This
research utilized NLP tools to quantify the effect from news articles by the New
York Times, thus translating them into quantitative weights. This research utilized Valence Aware Dictionary and Sentiment Reasoner (VADER), a subset of
NLP technology designed to analyze the polarity of short texts, and TextBlob,
a Python library used to perform NLP tasks [1]. VADER is based on a lexicon that measures the effect of text by matching sentence features to sentiment
scores. Since it was designed to evaluate short sentences, VADER was used to
analyze article headlines, while TextBlob was used to analyse news artice content.
Ultimately, the model returned the mean squared error (MSE) values of 0.00109,
0.00497 and 0.03526 (the closer the MSE value is to 0, the better the model’s
performance) when attempting to predict the closing stock price of Amazon, Microsoft and Tesla respectively [1].

Bharti et al. [29] employed a similar approach to Guo [1] in their study. They
utilized a data crawler to collect a rich dataset consisting of news headlines and
the corresponding market prices, thus enabling a holistic examination of the relationship between news sentiments and stock market behavior. Sentiment analysis
of the news headlines was also conducted using VADER [29]. Following this, the
sentiment-enhanced dataset was utilized in two separate prediction models. Firstly,
XGBoost was employed for classification purposes to anticipate stock price movements (bullish or bearish). Secondly, Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) was used
for more accurate stock price predictions [29]. In terms of performance, LSTM
exhibited a Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) of 4.164%, while XGBoost
achieved an accuracy score of 0.945. Accuracy score quantifies the model’s cor11

Chapter 2. Literature Review

rectness in predicting outcomes in relation to the total number of predictions
made [29].

Other studies by Shah et al. [10] and Dang et al. [11] both analyzed the effects
of news on the stock market with use of a dictionary-based sentiment analysis model. The use of a sentiment analysis dictionary allowed the researchers
to achieve a directional accuracy of 70.59% and 73% respectively, in short-term
stock price movement prediction [10] [11]. In addition to this, Shah et al. [10]
mentioned the implementation of a word-weighting approach in the analysis pipeline
as part of their future research. This technique assigns the appropriate weight to
each term based on their impact to the stock price. Overall, the findings [10]
[11] [1] suggest that news analysis can be an effective approach for predicting
stock prices and stock price movement.

2.6.3

Social Media Sentiment Analysis

The evolution of social media has created a new meaningful channel for users
to share information and their personal feelings. Opinions posted on social media
platforms depict the sentimental state of several users [30]. These users express
their opinions for a company or its products in tweets or comments. The extraction of sentiments from these comments is used to identify the user’s view
for a particular product or company [31]. Hence, machine learning researchers
exploited this wealth of information.

In a study by Liu et al. [32], the researchers measured the synergy between
investor sentiment and stock price movement. NLP techniques were utilized to
extract opinions from China’s largest social media platform for stock market investors. The researchers [32] found that investor sentiment has a weak correlation with stock prices. These findings are consistent with those of Behrendt
and Schmidt [33] who used Twitter sentiment to measure investors’ interest in
12

Chapter 2. Literature Review

individual-level stocks. They claimed that the incorporation of information extracted from Twitter into intraday prediction models for return volatility is unlikely to have a substantial impact on prediction performance [33].

Moreover, Dhaoui et al. [30] identified numerous limitations in their evaluation
of the performance of two automated social media sentiment analysis approaches
(lexicon-based and machine learning). Such limitations include informal language,
contextual understanding, misspelling, ironic language and sarcasm. Hence, while
NLP has made significant progress, accurate analysis of social media content remains a formidable task due to the language used on these platforms [30].

2.7

Machine Learning Algorithms

Machine learning encompasses various algorithms that enable machines to identify patterns autonomously, thus eliminating the need for it to be programmed
with specific instructions [34]. Machine learning (ML) has become an increasingly popular technique for predicting stock movements. This approach uses historical stock data to train models that can identify patterns and trends that may
indicate future movements in the stock market [35]. The accuracy of these predictions can vary depending on the amount and quality of the data used to train
the models, as well as the complexity of the algorithms used [36].

2.7.1

Artificial Neural Networks

Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) are designed to replicate the neural networks
in the human brain [34] [37] [38].

ANNs consist of interconnected neurons

and are composed of multiple layers of nodes, including input and output layers specifically designed for data input and result collection, respectively [38].
13

Chapter 2. Literature Review

The signals between the interconnected nodes carry weights, which are adjusted
during the training process to enhance the neural network’s performance [37].

Figure 2.2: Artificial Neural Network

The ANN passes input data through the network’s nodes, which rely on the incoming signal’s weights in order to make predictions. The network is trained by
utilizing training data and random weight parameters to enable the network to
generate provisional output values [38]. Once the signals are fed through the input layer, they are transmitted through the hidden nodes, where their weights are
adjusted, in order to determine the output values [37]. Subsequently, the output
values are compared to correct values, and a self-learning process referred to as
back-propagation is employed to adjust the weights accordingly, if the outputs are
not within a predetermined threshold. This process is repeated until the network’s
generated outputs meet the specified threshold [37]. Conversely, excessive training

a problem in the application of stock price prediction [39]. Moreover, Yoo et

adaptive learning abilities that make them increasingly useful in the field of fi-

2.7.2

Support Vector Machines

Support Vector Machine (SVM) is a machine learning algorithm, used for classification and regression tasks, that aims to find a hyperplane in a higher-dimensional
space to separate classes [40]. Huang et al. [41] conducted a study on financial movement prediction using SVM to forecast the weekly direction of the
NIKKEI 225 index. In this study, the researchers found that SVM outperformed
other classification methods such as Elman Backpropagation Neural Networks,
Quadratic Discriminant Analysis and Linear Discriminant Analysis [41] [40]. Similarly, Kim [42] utilized SVM to predict the daily stock price change direction
in the Korea stock price index (KOSPI). The results demonstrated that SVM surpassed both Backpropagation Neural Network and Case-Based Reasoning, highlighting its potential as an alternative approach to improve stock market prediction
accuracy [42].

2.7.3

K-means

The K-means algorithm is an unsupervised clustering technique that aims to classify unlabeled data into distinct groups, referred to as clusters [43]. Objects are
grouped into clusters based on their similarity, while dissimilar objects are assigned to separate clusters. This process ensures the maximization of intra-class
similarity while minimizing inter-class similarity [44]. The partitioning process
starts by randomly assigning data objects to different partitions. Each iteration
sees objects transferred to another partition, with a centroid, which is the average
of all data objects within it, representing each partition. Each partition must have
at least one data object, and every object is assigned to exactly one cluster [43].

15

Chapter 2. Literature Review

K-means is widely used due to its ability to effectively handle various types of
data [43] [44]. The algorithm starts by selecting k classes with k reference points
as initial estimates. Then the data is partitioned into k distinct regions based on
their respective distances from the reference points [44]. In addition, each region is assessed using the reference point as its centroid, and the data points are
clustered based on their nearest neighbour. The algorithm then employs iterative
adjustments of the initial reference points, minimizing the distance between them
and their respective members [44].

2.7.4

Hybrid Models

According to Wu et al. [45], not all machine learning technologies are equally
effective for specific tasks. Hence, to overcome the limitations of single-method
algorithms, hybrid machine learning techniques (which combine different mechanisms) have been adopted [45]. In Bahrammirzaee’s [46] study, it was indicated
that the combination of multiple machine learning techniques into hybrid models
has led to improved performance, when compared to single-method techniques, in
the domain of financial prediction. In stock prediction systems, hybrid techniques
can utilize both simple and complex elements, thus guaranteeing that only the
most relevant features are considered, leading to improved efficiency and accuracy of the hybrid system [47].

3.1

Introduction

This chapter outlines the quantitative methods used to attempt to answer the following research questions:

• What level of accuracy is achievable when machine learning is applied to
technical and fundamental strategies in predicting future stock prices?
• Does custom NLP-ML integrated technology perform better than off the
shelf components in stock price prediction?

Authors [1] [32] [8] have proposed various machine learning approaches that utilize financial data to identify patterns and tentatively predict the direction or even
the price of the stock market. Therefore, several factors were considered when
selecting the specific approach for this study. Being a form of technical analysis, pattern recognition only considers quantifiable data, thus overlooking several
factors such as news, conferences and discussions, all of which can majorly influence investors’ attitudes toward the market [3] [21]. Conversely, the efficacy
of NLP technology when applied to the extraction of social media sentiment is
hindered by the use of informal language, contextual understanding, misspelling,
18

Chapter 3. Research Methodology

ironic language and sarcasm [30]. In addition, [33] [32] claimed that investor
sentiment is weakly correlated to stock fluctuation, thus unlikely to have a significant impact on the performance of prediction models.

On the contrary, It has been established that a correlation exists between the publication of financial news and stock price fluctuations [27] [28]. Researchers [10]
[11] [1] have shown that the analysis of financial news content, using NLP, is an
effective approach in the prediction of stock prices and movement. Hence, this
research will contribute to the body of knowledge by investigating the potential
benefits of integrating machine learning techniques with financial news analysis to
predict stock prices, with the anticipation that similar technology can be applied
to other investment products.

To address the research questions above, this research proposes the incorporation
of machine learning and NLP techniques to predict the actual stock prices of specific stocks, by analysing publicly available news articles and stock prices from
Yahoo Finance. It is anticipated that market reactions vary across different types

providing potential investors with a tool that delivers organized and centralized

3.2.1

Data Storage

Given the rapidly fluctuating stock market environment, predictive models for
stock price predictions necessitate significant speed. However, the extensive volume of textual data required for this research poses a challenge to relational
databases, which are specifically designed for structured data. Consequently, the
proposed technology employs a data storage engine in lieu of a relational database,

maintaining selected data cached in memory for fast access. Indexes are used

data utilization while minimizing disk access, thereby enhancing the performance

high performance indexing architecture for real-time online audio searching systems. By serializing data to disk, the researchers [49] achieved enhanced query
performance and reduced memory usage.

A custom data storage and processing engine was implemented as a substitute

To facilitate the storage and manipulation of the data, three classes were designed

Machine Learning

In order to quantify the effect of a news article on a particular stock, a hybrid
machine learning algorithm was adopted. This algorithm borrows the weighting
and clustering concepts from ANNs and K-Means classifiers, respectively. Since
market reactions differ across different types of news and stocks [48], the algorithm must operate on a per-stock level, thus training and prediction are done
individually for each stock saved within the system.

Training

During the data processing phase, two invocation options are available: individual
processing for a single news item or collective processing for all news currently
stored in the database. At this stage, the news article content has undergone
cleaning procedures, resulting in the removal of HTML syntax and advertising
material. Consequently, only the essential news content remains.

Figure 3.10: News Form

33

Chapter 3. Research Methodology

The process begins by searching the news article content for tickers, which are
typically enclosed within parentheses and of a short length. Once such content
is found, it is checked for the presence of a colon (”:”) or a period (”.”), as
well as verifying if all letters are in uppercase. If any of these criteria are met,
the code considers the text within parentheses as a ticker symbol, and the stock
crawling component utilizing the Yahoo Finance Search endpoint API is initiated.
If new stock tickers are found, the latter part of the stock crawler component is
invoked, which extracts data relevant to those stocks from their respective Yahoo
Finance webpage and saves them. This allows the tool to continuously learn
and expand its data set, enhancing its ability to identify patterns associated with
different stocks.

In the subsequent phase of the training process, the aforementioned hybrid machine learning algorithm is employed. The news article content is initially stored
in a 2D array, with the first dimension containing the sentences and the second
dimension containing the words found within the respective sentence. NER techniques are then applied to identify the occurrences of stock names, from those
currently stored in the database, within the sentences. Once detected, each word
in the corresponding sentence’s array is assigned a weight based on the price
change experienced by the specific stock on the day following the news article’s
publication date, hence highlighting the importance of having up-to-date data before initiating this process.

The WordANN instance associated with the mentioned stock is then retrieved.
This instance contains records of different words that have been previously detected during the training process, pertaining to the mentioned stock. If words
that are not currently present in the WordANN instance are discovered, a new
record is created with the previously assigned weight. Conversely, if the word
found is already stored in the instance, the previously assigned weight value is
added to that of the pre-existing record, and its respective divisor (the total number of a specific word’s occurrences, for a particular stock) is incremented. The
34

Chapter 3. Research Methodology

changes are then saved, with the expectation that the newfound data will further
enhance the mechanism’s performance.

Prediction

Before the prediction is invoked, a stock and prediction date must be specified. Once invoked, the WordANN and StockPriceHistoryTimeSeries objects are
retrieved.

Next, the last trading day before the prediction date is determined by subtracting one day from the prediction date, excluding weekends. While this does not
include bank holidays or other days on which trading is performed, it is considered enough for research purposes. The latest entry in the stock’s price history
for the last trading day is then obtained, from which the percentage change in
price compared to the previous trading day is calculated.

The algorithm proceeds to search for news articles between the last trading day
and the prediction date. It iterates through each news article, processes its content
by splitting it into a 2D array of sentences and their respective words, similar to
the training phase.

It then searches for occurrences of the stock name in each sentence and calculates a predicted change based on the weights associated with the words found in
the sentences. The weights are obtained from the WordANN objects that are created and refined throughout the testing phase. The predicted change is calculated
as the mean of the weights from the cluster of words found from the sentences
that mention the specified stock, similar to a K-Means algorithm.

After processing all news articles, the prototype calculates an average predicted
change by dividing the accumulated predicted change by the total number of
35

Chapter 3. Research Methodology

words counted. It adds the predicted change to the percentage change observed
in the stock price on the last trading day, assuming that price changes usually
have some inertia from one trading day to the next. In stocks, inertia refers to
a phenomenon where the prices of stocks don’t immediately respond to new information or changes in the market [54]. Finally, the predicted stock price is
calculated and added to the list of predictions, which are displayed on the UI.

[1], it is deemed to be enough for research purposes. The model was started
from a blank slate, i.e. 0 stocks, 0 stock exchanges, 0 news articles; and it
was then left to crawl and process news articles, creating stocks, currencies and
stock exchanges in real time as it discovers them. Neither Guo [1] nor Bharti et
al. [29] mention whether their proposed technology automatically discovers stocks
etc., or whether they were manually input.

4.3.2

Sample Sizes

Bharti et al. [29] did not specify the stock sample used in their research, while
Guo [1] used a sample of 3 stocks, namely Amazon, Microsoft and Tesla. Bharti
et al. [29] used a dataset of 10000 sentences to train the sentiment detection
module. This research opted for a broader sample of 221 stocks from the XETRA stock exchange. This has allowed a broader evaluation of the proposed
technology since some stocks might follow distinctive patterns and thus be less
challenging to predict than others.

Another difference in the proposed technology and that put forward by both
Bharti et al. [29] and Guo [1] is that this research proposes an increasingly more
granular machine learning structure that allows article sentiment to differentiate
between one stock and another. This allows different terms and words to have
different meanings in conjunction with different stocks. In contrast, both Bharti
et al. [29] and Guo [1] concentrated more on the extraction of pure sentiment
out of the news articles themselves, without taking into account the actual stock.

4.3.3

Execution Performance

The proposed proof of concept implementation uses a custom data storage engine,
that allows frequently used data to be cached, and all data to be indexed at
will. Data searching is done using indexes where available, while QuickSort is
used as the main sorting algorithm. This has resulted in a very good overall
execution speed, which is pivotal considering the vast amount of processing that
is necessary to train the model and to calculate predictions. 50,000 news articles
were processed in under 7 days. Taking into account that every HTTP request
is followed by a 4-5 second pause to avoid making too many requests to the
Yahoo Finance servers in a short time, and that the model started from no data
at all, this is considered a very good performance. During this time, 500Mb
of news article data has been downloaded and analysed, while another 4.6Gb of
stock price history data has also been acquired and analysed, pertaining to 19675
stocks. The resulting machine learning models totalled 404Mb.

Guo [1] explains that the actual proof of concept implementations were done
in Python, relying heavily on readily available libraries. However Guo [1] does
not state the actual time taken to train the model or to predict the future stock
values. For this reason, no comparison could be made in terms of execution
speed.

In similar fashion, Bharti et al. [29] explain that they used VADER for the sentiment analysis, and LTSM for the prediction of stock prices; but no details are
given as to how it performed or the time taken to train the model and calculate
predictions.

5.1

Conclusion

This research has concluded that while a considerable amount of previous research exists in the area of stock prediction, most of it simply targets price
movements (i.e. whether the price will go up or down) rather than attempting to
predict the actual price. Prediction accuracies vary, but most indicate that there
is room for improvement. This research posed the following research questions:

• What level of accuracy is achievable when machine learning is applied to technical and fundamental strategies in predicting future stock prices?

This research has shown that MAPE levels of 0.131% to 10.059% are achievable
using the technology being proposed. It is also probable that better results could
be achieved if the model is trained with more data and over a longer period of
time.

• Does custom NLP-ML integrated technology perform better than off the shelf
components in stock price prediction?

Taking the custom components proposed by this research and the readily available

46

Chapter 5. Conclusions and Recommendations

components used by Bharti et al. [29], the balance tips towards acknowledging
that custom machine learning and NLP components can effectively increase the
prediction performance.

5.2

Limitations and Recommendations

Being a proof of concept tool, the proposed technology was developed purely
for research purposes. Hence, several features can be improved, built upon and
even added, to further enhance its performance. It is anticipated that the proposed technology, which is currently limited to stocks, has the potential to be
applied to other investment products such as Forex, Bonds, and Cryptocurrency.
By expanding the prototype to include these investment products, the effectiveness of the technology across different markets within the financial domain can
be assessed, whilst providing investors with additional opportunities.

Due to time constraints, the data used in the research was limited to a three-year
period. However, the trends emerging from the results indicate that the accuracy of predicted prices could be further improved by evaluating the technology
with larger datasets. Different time periods can exhibit different levels of market volatility, investor sentiment, and economic conditions. Hence, access to a
more extensive range of news article content from different periods would enable
the machine learning model to capture a broader range of market dynamics and
potentially enhance its predictive capabilities.

In this research, the proposed implementation was tested over a 30-day period,
also due to time constraints. The selection of this period was based on the observation of a high density of news articles published each day within the available dataset. It would be beneficial to explore how the technology performs over
longer testing periods to assess its robustness and reliability in different market

The implementation of an automated crawling and training sequence would greatly
benefit the technology’s continuous learning process. This would involve the automatic collection and processing of data related to stocks, their price history,
news articles, and the impact of news on stocks. By eliminating the need for
manual intervention, the machine learning model can continually expand its data
set, improving its understanding of market dynamics and enhancing the accuracy
of predictions.

The introduction of notification features, such as email or SMS alerts, would provide investors with timely information on favorable trading conditions for specific
stocks. By notifying investors when the conditions align for profitable trades, the
technology can increase the likelihood of making informed investment decisions
at the right time, potentially leading to higher profitability.

The inclusion of more diverse data sets, such as social media platforms and investor forums, can provide valuable input to the machine learning component.
These sources can offer real-time sentiment analysis, further enhancing the technology’s ability to predict stock prices accurately.
