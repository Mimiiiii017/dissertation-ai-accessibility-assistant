Abstract

Abstract

In this study, the immersive exploration of the campuses of MCAST and University of Malta (UOM) through a digital game is investigated. The digital game
incorporates realistic artificial intelligence, beautifully rendered non-player characters, and meticulously designed construction environments. The main goal of this
research is to assist a specific student in locating their ideal campus and course.
The project aims to provide users with an unparalleled opportunity to engage in
an interactive educational experience facilitated by the power of Unreal Engine.
Through careful investigation, various educational opportunities within this project
have been discovered, allowing users to adapt and grow. The outcomes of the
study will be evaluated using a quantitative approach to assess the success of the
digital game. A group of 100 individuals will complete a pre-questionnaire before
participating in the game, while another group of 100 individuals will complete
a post-questionnaire after their engagement. This strategy will provide insightful information regarding the influence and efficacy of the immersive exploration
on the participants’ preferences and educational options. In terms of future research, this digital game can be converted into a virtual reality (VR) game as
a cutting-edge approach in an effort to push the boundaries of educational discovery. With this in mind, we can explore the potential of this game-changing
technology by immersing players in an exploration of the MCAST and University
of Malta (UOM) campuses. Another future change which could be implemented,
instead of doing a quantitative approach of 100 people questionnaires, interviews
could be held in order to unlock a new dimension of understanding and provide
participants with a more authentic and engaging research experience.

1.1

Aims & Objectives

The study of research is based on Recreating School for a user’s purpose. Students frequently find it tough to distinguish the distinctive qualities and experiences linked with each institution while evaluating several schools and their offers. Due to a lack of information, students may find it difficult to make educated decisions, which causes students to choose educational paths that do not
meet their needs and objectives. The motivation for this research came from a
personal experience when I didn’t have a clue on what course I wanted to pursue and no real explanation and difference was shown from all the institutions
I researched. This research study offers an innovative solution to this problem
which is an immersive digital campus made with Unreal Engine that features the
main institutions of the University Of Malta (UOM) and Mcast.
The goal of this research project is to determine the degree of user satisfaction
and judgment with the obstacles in the digital game. The study intends to assess the success of the game in satisfying user expectations, improving decisionmaking abilities, and offering an enjoyable and gratifying experience by analyzing
user input and impressions. Finally, this thesis investigates the general issue of
how a digital game can help students make decisions. The game acts as a tool to
equip students with the required knowledge and experiential learning opportunities
that enhance decision-making by submerging players in a dynamic and interactive

The main hypothesis motivating this research study is that by giving students the
chance to explore and traverse different digital campuses, they will develop a
better grasp of the distinctive qualities and educational opportunities of each institution. Users will be able to immerse themselves in a digital video game world
that accurately represents how the campuses function in real life by assuming the
role of a student character within the game. By allowing them to become familiar with the daily routines, academic lessons, and course-specific characteristics of
each institution, this experiential learning strategy hopes to aid students in their
decision-making process.

Game Development and Game Engines

The start of Game Development

The game ”Maze War,” which was developed in the early 1970s, is usually regarded as having the first gaming engine. First-person shooter game Maze War

8

Chapter 2. Literature Review

offered multiplayer participation in a common virtual setting. The software used
in Maze War’s game engine was a framework for the game’s mechanics, including graphic rendering, player input management, and player network connectivity.
A group of computer scientists at NASA’s Ames Research Center, including Steve
Colley, Bruce Baumgart, and others, developed the engine. Although it was primarily created for research objectives, it immediately became well-liked and frequently used by computer enthusiasts. Following Maze War, game engines continued to develop, gaining new features for networking, physics, and visuals. From
straightforward mobile games to intricate AAA titles, a variety of video games
are being created using game engines. Unity, Unreal Engine, and CryEngine are
a few examples of well-known gaming engines.

Guner Koc Aytekin [4] mentioned that every single day, advancements and breakthroughs in our age of globalization are reported on behalf of knowledge. Since

always changing and expanding in novel and fascinating ways. Technology developments, such as quicker processors, better graphics capabilities, and more potent
game engines, are what drive the industry. The growth of independent games is
one of the largest trends in game production right now. Instead of huge game
production studios or individual creators, these games are created by smaller, independent studios. Indie games frequently emphasize distinctive gameplay me9

The main topic discussed regarding the future of game development has to be the
metaverse. Players in a persistent world that keeps changing even when they are

of a metaverse aims to blur the distinction between the actual world and the virtual world by offering a completely immersive and compelling experience. Recent
years have seen a substantial increase in interest in the idea of a metaverse, with
big internet firms like Facebook and Microsoft investing heavily in its creation.
