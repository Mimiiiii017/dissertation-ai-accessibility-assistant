3.6  Ethical Considerations
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.6.1  Participant Anonymity, Data Privacy, and Secure Storage
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The study adopts a privacy-by-design approach consistent with the General Data
Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) and the MCAST Research Ethics Policy.
No personally identifiable information is collected beyond what is necessary to
coordinate the study sessions and verify participant eligibility.

Each participant will be assigned a randomly generated alphanumeric code at the
point of recruitment.  All task-performance data, NASA-TLX questionnaire
responses, and observer field notes will be associated exclusively with this
pseudonymous code.  The mapping table linking participant names to codes will be
stored separately from the study data, password-protected, and made accessible
only to the principal researcher.

Audio recordings taken during the debrief interview will be transcribed in full
and subsequently deleted.  The transcript will be pseudonymised before archiving
by replacing all references to the participant's name, employer, or identifying
project details with coded placeholders.  No identifying excerpts will be
included in any published output; any quotations used in the dissertation will
include only the participant code, role category (e.g. front-end developer), and
years of experience.

All study data will be retained for a minimum of one year after submission of the
dissertation and for a maximum of three years, in accordance with MCAST research
data retention policy.  Data will be stored on a password-protected institutional
device with full-disk encryption.  No data will be stored on personal cloud
services or shared devices.

The extension itself, as a static analysis tool operating on files open in the
developer's editor, does not transmit any source code to external parties other
than the configured LLM relay endpoint (localhost:11434 in the default
configuration).  In the deployment configuration used for this study, all
inference requests are routed to a self-hosted Ollama instance; no source code
leaves the participant's machine during evaluation sessions.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.6.2  Informed Consent and Institutional Research Approval
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

All participants will receive a written participant information sheet at least 48
hours before their scheduled session.  The information sheet describes: the
purpose of the study; the tasks participants will be asked to perform; the data
that will be collected and how it will be stored; the pseudonymisation procedure;
the basis on which participation is voluntary; and the fact that participants may
withdraw at any point during the session without giving a reason and without
penalty.

Written informed consent will be obtained from every participant at the start of
the session, prior to any data collection.  The consent form will explicitly
address: (a) audio recording during the debriefing phase; (b) use of anonymised
excerpts in the final dissertation and any future academic publications; and (c)
acknowledgement that participation is voluntary and that withdrawal rights are
unconditional.  Participants who withdraw after the session commences will have
their data removed from the dataset and destroyed.

The study protocol has been submitted to the MCAST Institute of Information and
Communication Technology Research Ethics Committee for institutional approval
before any recruitment commences.  The submission covers participant eligibility
criteria, recruitment materials, the participant information sheet and consent
form, the observation protocol, and the data management plan described above.
Research activities will not begin until written approval has been received.

No deception is involved at any stage of the study.  Participants are informed
that they will evaluate an AI-powered accessibility assistant and that their
task performance and subjective workload ratings will be measured.  There is no
manipulation of beliefs or suppression of information that would require
debriefing.

As the extension analyses only files already in the participant's editor and
produces informational diagnostic output, there is no plausible pathway for harm
within the tool's operation.  Psychological risk is minimal: the tasks involve
reviewing developer-familiar source code and completing a validated workload
questionnaire.  Should a participant express distress at any point, the session
will be paused and the researcher will follow the MCAST student and participant
welfare protocol.
