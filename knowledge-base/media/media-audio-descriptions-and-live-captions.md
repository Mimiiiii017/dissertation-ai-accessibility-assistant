# Media: Audio Descriptions and Live Captions

## Tags
Tags: #media #audio-descriptions #live-captions #blind #low-vision #wcag #1.2.3 #1.2.4 #1.2.5 #eaa

## Purpose
Ensure video content is accessible to blind and low-vision users through audio descriptions of visual information, and that live video content includes real-time captions for Deaf and hard-of-hearing users.

## Key points

### Audio Descriptions (WCAG 1.2.3, 1.2.5)
- Audio descriptions narrate important visual information that is not conveyed through the existing soundtrack (actions, scene changes, on-screen text, facial expressions).
- A media alternative (full text description) can be provided instead of audio descriptions for prerecorded video at Level A (1.2.3).
- At Level AA (1.2.5), audio descriptions are required for prerecorded video with audio.
- Audio descriptions are inserted during natural pauses in the dialogue.
- Extended audio descriptions pause the video to allow longer narration when natural pauses are insufficient.
- Audio descriptions must be synchronised with the video content.

### Live Captions (WCAG 1.2.4)
- Live video content with audio must have real-time captions (WCAG 1.2.4, Level AA).
- Live captions may be generated by a human captioner (CART — Communication Access Realtime Translation) or by automated speech-to-text with human monitoring.
- Live captions are essential for webinars, live streams, virtual meetings, and live events.
- Accuracy is critical — automated-only live captions should be reviewed for quality.

## Developer checks
- Identify all prerecorded video content and check for audio descriptions or a full text alternative.
- Verify audio descriptions describe key visual information not available in the audio track.
- Confirm live video events include real-time captions.
- Check that audio description tracks can be toggled on and off by users.
- Test that the media player supports multiple audio tracks (original + audio-described).
- Verify live caption quality and accuracy.

## Fix patterns
- Create a separate audio description track and add it as an alternative audio track.
- Provide a full text transcript that includes visual descriptions as an alternative to audio descriptions at Level A.
- Use the `<track>` element with `kind="descriptions"` for audio descriptions in HTML5 video.
- For live events, engage a CART provider or use high-quality automated captioning with human oversight.
- Ensure the media player UI allows users to toggle audio descriptions and captions.
- Provide a separate audio-described version of the video if the player does not support multiple tracks.

## Examples
```html
<!-- Video with audio description track -->
<video controls>
  <source src="product-demo.mp4" type="video/mp4">
  <track kind="captions" src="demo-captions.vtt" srclang="en" label="English captions">
  <track kind="descriptions" src="demo-descriptions.vtt" srclang="en" label="Audio descriptions">
</video>

<!-- Alternative: link to audio-described version -->
<video controls>
  <source src="product-demo.mp4" type="video/mp4">
  <track kind="captions" src="demo-captions.vtt" srclang="en" label="English captions">
</video>
<p>
  <a href="product-demo-described.mp4">Watch version with audio descriptions</a>
</p>

<!-- Full text alternative (Level A alternative to audio descriptions) -->
<video controls>
  <source src="product-demo.mp4" type="video/mp4">
</video>
<details>
  <summary>Full text description of video</summary>
  <div>
    <p><strong>00:00</strong> – The video opens with a wide shot of a modern office. 
      A person is sitting at a desk using a laptop.</p>
    <p><strong>00:10</strong> – The speaker says: "Welcome to our product demo." 
      On screen, the application dashboard is displayed showing three charts.</p>
    <p><strong>00:25</strong> – The speaker clicks the "Reports" menu item. 
      A dropdown appears with four options: Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Custom.</p>
  </div>
</details>
```
